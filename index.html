The major advances in education are yet to happen

We currently default to the approaches of teachers, acting as knowledge intermediaries. In the diverse, fast-changing world of business, this can be unnecessarily slow. We often fight against irrelevance to founders' current challenges and context.

The copy-paste flatness of one-way education will give rise to multi-faceted, peer-to-peer methods that focus on the learner, not the teacher. Collective learn by doing.

True Ecosystems

Accelerators have taken a very visible role in startup ecosystems, but the narrow focus of the accelerator model is leading most communities away from other opportunities. At the moment all accelerators use the same selection criteria: who has the greatest ambition to grow.

This analysis points out that high-growth startup ecosystems typically have more diverse roots, and describes strategic models for investment from a community leader and governement perspective.

No more hubs: startup community is P2P
Startup capitals are an out-dated concept. Global startup culture is becoming more networked and less top-down. The fastest-growing startup communities aren't trying to be the best-in-region or playing a zero-sum game, they're embracing the connections to other places. At a global level, our community behaviour is peer-to-peer. Centralisation is becoming a liability; rivalries get in the way far too often.

Our History

  • 2010

  • Leancamp launched. Creates the first strong links from Lean Startup to The Business Model Canvas and User Experience Design. Europe's introduction to Lean Startup.

  • 2011

  • Leanspark - the first European Lean Startup Conference, held in Sofia, Bulgaria

  • Leancamp expands to Amsterdam

  • 2012

  • Founder Centric created to raise the level of startup education in Europe. We deliver most of the education for almost all of Europe's first accelerators, from London, to Tallinn, to Sofia.

  • Leancamp expands to Barcelona and Berlin.

  • 2013

  • Leancamp expands to Rotterdam and Cologne, partners with Pirate Summit.

  • Official release of The Mom Test, Founder Centric's contribution to Customer Development.

  • 2014

  • Founder Centric moves from training to programme design. We design mentoring programmes, Masters' curricula, accelerators and global entrepreneurship prizes for: Unilever, UCL, Oxford University, Orange, Barclays Bank, The Royal Academy Of Engineering.

  • Now running in 15 cities, including San Francisco in collaboration with The Lean Startup Conference.

  • Digital Business Academy launches, with Tech City, Cambridge and UCL.

  • Lean Startup Circle adopts P2P formats globally, based on Leancamp. Leancamp's peer-to-peer formats now runs around the globe.

  • Village Accelerator launches in Sofia, Bulgaria.

  • 2015

  • Source Institute founded

    Officially uniting all of these programmes and founders under a common goal: empower founders from all over the globe through peer-to-peer startup education.

  • The Sources launches in Sub-Saharan Africa

    An online course for African startups that shares practical advice on Africa's unique environment.

Our Services

Startup Workshops

Workshops that first lay the groundwork for mindset shift, then layer on practical skills. We cover Team Alignment, The Growth Mindset, Lean Startup, Business Model Design, Learning From Customers, Decision Making and more.

Accelerator Design

We designed most of the first European accelerator programmes, and moved on to apply the accelerator model in new contexts - creative business, engineers in Africa, cleantech and beyond. We're the authors of The Accelerator Design Guide, and numerous tools used in accelerators around the world.

Pre-accelerators

We designed and delivered Europe's best performing pre-accelerator programmes, with 50% entering accelerators or raising investment and 70+% launching viable businesses.

Train The Trainer

Our unique approach to entrepreneur education is shared with our university and accelerator partners. We've taught programme directors for the European Union, university professors across Western Europe, and a significant portion of trainers in the accelerator industry.

Mentor Training

We wrote the book on startup mentoring, Mentor Impact, which was developed by interviewing the top mentors in the US and Europe. We ourselves have been long-term mentors and advisors to startups in accelerators and in later growth phases.

Unconferences

We're the pioneers in practical unconferences, having started Leancamp in 2010, which developed the Lean Startup methodology globally. We've used this format successful in accelerators and universities all over Europe, including Oxford and UCL.

Immersion

Give your students a 48-hour taste of life as an entrepreneur, and practical skills training to move forward with their startup. Startup Immersion is a hands-on experience covering the muddy exploration and hard decisions that founders face when getting their ideas off the ground.

Creative Accelerator

Make your art pay your rent. A 3-week, part-time programme that enables creative people to make a viable business out of their talents. Explores and makes sense of their possibilities, looking at clever ways artists make money, and moves them to run fun social experiments that reveal a commercial path.
